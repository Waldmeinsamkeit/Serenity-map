# Market Source Playbook

Use this when market-specific evidence matters.

## A-Shares

Primary paths:

- Annual, interim, quarterly, and ad hoc announcements.
- Shanghai/Shenzhen/Beijing exchange inquiry letters and replies.
- Hudongyi / SSE e-interaction investor Q&A.
- Tenders, winning bids, customer certification, patents, standards.
- Environmental approvals, energy approvals, project filings, land and capacity records.
- Financial fields: revenue mix, gross margin, receivables, inventory, contract liabilities, operating cash flow, capex, related-party transactions.

Provider/helper paths:

- Use `references/data-source-urls.md` to choose public/provider URLs for CNINFO, Eastmoney, Tushare, iWencai, Tencent, Baidu, Sina, and news/research feeds.
- Prefer official announcements and exchange documents for primary proof; use structured market APIs for numeric fields and leads.

## Hong Kong

Primary paths:

- HKEX filings, annual/interim reports, circulars, placings, connected transactions.
- Southbound eligibility and liquidity.
- Mainland policy exposure and customer concentration.
- Company IR, earnings materials, credible media.

## US

Primary paths:

- SEC filings, 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, S-1/S-3, proxy statements.
- Earnings calls and transcripts.
- Investor presentations.
- Customer concentration, backlog, inventory, capex, margin, dilution/ATM risk.
- Patent, standard, regulator, import/export, and specialist trade sources.

## Taiwan, Japan, Korea, Europe

Primary paths:

- Local exchange filings.
- Monthly revenue or operating data where available.
- Company IR and integrated reports.
- Segment disclosure, trade journals, export statistics.
- FX, geopolitics, customer concentration, and local policy exposure.

## Cross-Market Rule

The economic logic transfers across markets. The evidence path changes. When uncertain, state the exact source path that still needs checking.
